About Virupaksha Temple

Virupaksha Temple, located in Hampi, Karnataka, is one of the oldest living temples in India, where worship has continued uninterrupted for more than 1,300 years. Dedicated to Lord Shiva, worshipped here as Virupaksha (the consort of Goddess Pampa), the temple holds immense spiritual and historical importance.

Temple Highlights

Oldest Active Temple

Historic Architecture

Magnificent Dravidian architecture built during the Vijayanagara Empire.

Sacred Tungabhadra River

The temple sits beside the holy Tungabhadra River, adding spiritual value.

UNESCO Heritage

Part of the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Hampi.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the temple opening hours?

The temple is open from 6:00 AM to 9:00 PM daily.

Is photography allowed inside?

Yes, photography is allowed in most areas except the sanctum.

Is there any dress code?

Modest traditional clothing is recommended for entry.

Are guides available?

Yes, licensed guides are available near the entrance.
